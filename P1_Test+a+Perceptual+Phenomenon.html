Project 1: Test a Perceptual Phenomenon¶

Background Information:¶

In a Stroop task, participants are presented with a list of words, with each word displayed in a color of ink. The participants task is to say out loud the color of the ink in which the word is printed. The task has two conditions: a congruent words condition, and an incongruent words condition. In the congruent words condition, the words being displayed are color words whose names match the colors in which they are printed: for example RED, BLUE. In the incongruent words condition, the words displayed are color words whose names do not match the colors in which they are printed: for example PURPLE, ORANGE. In each case, we measure the time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists. Each participant will go through and record a time from each condition.

Question 2¶

What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? What kind of statistical test do you expect to perform? Justify your choices.

A2: The null hypothesis is the time to name the ink colors in Congruent Word Conditions and the time to name the ink colors in InCongruent Word Conditions ARE EQUAL. The alternative hypothesis is the time to name the ink colors in Congruent Word Conditions and the time to name the ink colors in InCongruent Word Conditions ARE NOT EQUAL (either can be faster/slower).
The type of statistical test is Dependent t-test, with repeated measures design. We are having the same group of participants read two sets of words (two conditions applied to same sample participants).

\begin{equation*} H_{0}: \mu_{1} = \mu_{2} \text{where } \\\\ H_{a}: \mu_{1} \neq \mu_{2} \\\\ \text{where } H_{0} \text{ is the null hypothesis; } \\\\ \text{and } H_{a} \text{ is the alternative hypothesis; } \\\\ \text{and } \mu_{1} \text{ is the mean of congruent data set; } \\\\ \text{and } \mu_{2} \text{ is the mean of incongruent data set; } \\\\ \end{equation*}
